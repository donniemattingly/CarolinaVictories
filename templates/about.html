About Carolina Victories

What it is

Carolina Victories is a site that attempts to track the games individual members of the Marching Tar Heels have attended and the result of those games. I was always curious how many times I had played Victory and Hark the Sound after I watched one of the many athletic teams we play for win a game. Knowing this begs other questions. Who in the band has seen the most? Who has seen the least? Who seems to bring the most luck to a game? Who seems to bring the least? Carolina Victories tries to answer these questions.

What it is not

This site is neither exhaustive nor authoritative in its information. The data comes from GoHeels.com and Blue/White band lists distributed by Mr. Fuchs that I found in my email inbox from the past 4 years. Having only those sources to draw from there are many errors that can occur in such an analysis. If you swapped into/out of a gig, missed a gig, switched bands, or have a spelling error in your name on one of the lists the information represented on this site is likely to be incorrect. In addition, one of the list only contained games in the spring semester, so that introduces errors as well.

How it works

This site is built using Flask, a python microframework for the web. The code can be found on github. I used a piece of software that strips text from pdf files to more easily parse the Blue/White band lists. The data from those lists is used to assign individuals to ensembles and dates to individual ensembles. These dates are checked against data from GoHeels.com and that allows me to determine the result of each game. All the design was done by me (that's why it's so bad) using Illustrator and Bootstrap. You can contact me at donniewmattingly[at]gmail.com if you are having issues with the site.
